## Discover Gubbio: Umbria's Medieval Masterpiece

Nestled on the slopes of Monte Ingino in the northeastern part of Umbria, Gubbio stands as a stunning example of medieval architecture and Italian heritage. Known as one of the best-preserved medieval towns in Italy, Gubbio offers an authentic journey back in time, making it an ideal location for historical productions, fantasy settings, or any project seeking to capture the essence of Italy's rich past.

## A Living Medieval Tapestry

Gubbio's remarkably intact medieval structure provides an unparalleled backdrop:

- Imposing Palazzo dei Consoli, a 14th-century Gothic masterpiece
- The ancient Roman Teatro Romano, one of the best-preserved in Italy
- Narrow, winding streets lined with stone houses and noble palazzi
- Piazza Grande, a stunning suspended square with breathtaking views

These elements create an authentic medieval atmosphere perfect for period dramas, historical documentaries, or fantasy productions.

## Rich Cultural Heritage

Gubbio is steeped in traditions and cultural significance:

- The famous Corsa dei Ceri, a spectacular annual race held since the 12th century
- The Gubbio Tablets, ancient bronze plates with Umbrian inscriptions
- Palazzo Ducale, showcasing Renaissance architecture
- Numerous churches housing important artworks and frescoes

These cultural assets offer diverse storytelling opportunities, from historical reenactments to explorations of Italian traditions.

## Natural Beauty and Landscapes

The surrounding area offers diverse natural settings:

- The lush forests of Monte Ingino, accessible by a panoramic cable car
- The nearby Apennine Mountains, providing dramatic backdrops
- Gola del Bottaccione, a deep gorge with geological and historical significance
- Picturesque countryside dotted with olive groves and vineyards

This variety of landscapes allows for versatile outdoor shooting options, from rugged mountain scenes to serene rural vistas.

## Artisanal Crafts and Local Traditions

Gubbio is renowned for its artisanal heritage:

- Traditional ceramics workshops, producing the famous "Gubbio lustro"
- Ironwork and woodworking artisans
- Local food specialties, including the precious white truffle
- Christmas traditions, including the world's largest Christmas tree on Monte Ingino

These elements provide excellent opportunities for showcasing Italian craftsmanship and cultural practices.

## Cinematic Pedigree

Gubbio has already left its mark in the world of cinema:

- Featured in numerous Italian films and TV series
- Recognizable locations that have appeared in international productions
- A film-friendly community accustomed to hosting production crews

## Logistical Advantages

- Well-preserved medieval setting with minimal modern intrusions
- Proximity to other Umbrian attractions (Assisi, Perugia)
- Variety of unique indoor and outdoor locations within a compact area
- Local authorities experienced in facilitating film and photo shoots

## Why Choose Gubbio for Your Production?

- One of the best-preserved medieval towns in Italy
- Rich historical and cultural heritage spanning from ancient Roman times
- Unique annual traditions and festivals
- Diverse landscapes from mountain vistas to ancient urban settings
- Authentic Italian atmosphere with a strong sense of local identity
